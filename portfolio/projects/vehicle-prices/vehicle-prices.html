Pre-Owned Vehicle Sale Price Estimator Web-App

Project Summary

Developed a web-based tool that any sedan vehicle owner can use to get an estimate of what their vehicle would be worth based on various factors.

The service uses a trained XGBoost linear regression model, trained on historical vehicle data obtained from Kaggle, to make a vehicle sale price estimate.

The model was wrapped as a Flask Web Application and deployed to the internet using Heroku.

    Data Features

  • Year - year of manufacture.

  • Present Price - price of the vehicle if newly purchased from a dealership. ( In Indian Rupees (Lakhs)). Where 1 Lakh = 100,000 Rupees

  • Kms_Driven - the odometer in kilometers throughout the car’s use.

  • Fuel_Type - type of fuel the vehicle runs on (Diesel/Petrol/CNG)

  • Seller_Type - mode through which the vehicle is being sold (Dealer/Individual)

  • Transmission - vehicle transmission type (Automatic/Manual)

  • Owner - number of previous owners.

  • Selling_Price - the price that the pre-owned vehicle was actually sold for. (In Indian Rupees (Lakhs)). Where 1 Lakh = 100,000 Rupees

    • Important Note.

    All prices were converted from Indian Rupees to South African Rands (ZAR).

    Modelling

    The Selling_Price feature was the ground truth, leaving features 1-7 as the features from which the model would learn from.

    Four different estimators were experimented with for this project. They were:-

  • Random Forest Regressor
  • Vanilla Linear Regressor
  • Ridge Regressor
  • XGB Regressor (xgboost)

    • Evaluation Metrics

    A five-fold cross valdated R^@ and MSE score were used to evaluate the four models.

    Modelling Results

    Of the four fitted and evaluated estimators, the XGB Regressor model performed the best (metric-wise).


    Investigative Insights


    Feature Importance


    The feature importance analysis revealed:

    1. The Present_Price (current new vehicle sale price) is the most important feature.
    2. The Age of the vehicle was the second most important feature.
    3. The Kms_Driven by the vehicle was the third most important feature.

    Investigation Summary

    These findings fall right in line with the general vehicle value trends which are mainly determined by their depreciation due to their off-the-lot price, their age and mileage.
